Bio

Above all else, I am a scientist. In everything I do, I bring an analytic point of view, and strive for test-based solutions to all problems. I am a cloud-enthusiast, technologist, and curious academic.

I graduated from the University of Richmond in 2015 with a B.S. in chemistry, after which I went to work as a software engineer. In my time as an undergrad, I was a member of the Parish lab group and worked in computational quantum chemistry, making/calculating mathematical models of di-radical molecules resulting from the combustion of asphaltenes (found in petroleum products). The power and utility of programming became apparent to me while working in this group, and led me to learn more about this magic called computer science. After graduating, I was fortunate enough to be able to transition into a software engineering position.

In my free time, I enjoy working on pet projects (such as this website), reading about the latest advances in technology and sciences, investing, cooking, and playing sports.
